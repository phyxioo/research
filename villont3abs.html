Pechay (Brassica Rapa) is a prominent and ancient vegetable in the Philippines, valued for its nutritional benefits, including calcium, potassium, fiber, and Vitamin K. Known for its large edible terminal bud and leaves, Pechay is used both in cooking and as a remedy. The study investigates how varying water amounts affect Pechay growth, aiming to enhance its production efficiency for local farmers in response to high market demand.
